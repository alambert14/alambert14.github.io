Voltage Regulator Module/h1>

This component will be a quick one. The Voltage Regulator Module (VRM) isn't something that needs to be programmed, but it is important to know how it is necessary and what it can be used for. The VRM is a small component with a few ports that allows you to alter the voltage and current to meet the needs of certain electronics. The only voltage you will receive from the robot's battery is 12V. What if your Arduino, for example, only takes 5V? You couldn't imagine frying your Arduino! Fear not, because the VRM is here to help. Simply plug your Arduino into the 5V port and you are good to go. Having trouble finding the correct port? Just remember V = IR. Don't know what that means? Ask a sophomore or something (unless the new national science standards change who is and isn't allowed to take physics).
